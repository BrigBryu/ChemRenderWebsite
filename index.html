Free study tool · built by an ochem student

Turn AI organic chemistry answers into readable study cards.

ChemRender is a free browser tool for organic chemistry students. Use ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or a local model, then paste the response into ChemRender to study clean, readable molecule cards. No install needed — with a desktop app if you want easier clipboard import.

How it works

From an AI answer to study cards in five steps.

1

Open ChemRender

Use it free in your browser — or get the desktop app for easier clipboard import.

2

Copy the built-in prompt

Open the app and copy the built-in ChemRender prompt — no formatting to memorize.

3

Ask your AI tutor

Ask ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or another AI an organic chemistry question.

4

Paste the response

Paste the AI's answer back into ChemRender.

5

Study the cards

Review the rendered molecule cards with structures, charges, and captions.

What it does well

Readable chemistry cards for the topics students actually review.

  • Resonance forms
  • Lone pairs and formal charges
  • Functional groups
  • Stereochemistry
  • Line-structure practice

Functional groups

Built for studying

Keyboard shortcuts keep studying fast.

Shortcuts make import, replace, navigation, and export quick. Flexible keybinds let you use simple controls, Vim-style controls, or your own setup.

  • One-button import and paste workflow
  • Fast replace flow for fixing one card
  • Keyboard navigation between rendered cards
  • Flexible keybind presets and custom controls

ChemRender is free and works with the AI tools you already use. It is not another AI subscription and does not include an AI model. Bring ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or a local model — ChemRender just turns the answer into readable organic chemistry cards.
