How to use ChemRender

ChemRender renders AI organic chemistry answers into clean molecule cards. You bring the AI model you already use — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or a local model — and ChemRender handles the visuals.

The basic workflow

  1. Download and open ChemRender. Get the free app for macOS or Windows and launch it.
  2. Copy the built-in prompt. ChemRender includes a prompt that tells the AI how to format its answer. Copy it from inside the app — you do not need to write the format yourself.
  3. Ask an AI tutor an organic chemistry question. Paste the built-in prompt into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or whatever model you already use, then ask your question.
  4. Paste the AI response into ChemRender. Copy the answer and import it back into the app.
  5. Render and study the cards. ChemRender draws clean molecule cards with structures, lone pairs, formal charges, and captions.
  6. Open a card's details to see functional groups. Use the details on a card to inspect its functional groups when you want a closer look.

See resonance forms with electron arrows

ChemRender also includes an arrows add-on prompt. When you want to study resonance forms or mechanisms, copy the arrows prompt from inside the app along with the built-in prompt before asking your question. The AI will include electron-movement arrows, so the rendered cards show how electrons shift between resonance structures.

Use the AI you already have

ChemRender is not an AI service and does not sell a model. It works with whatever AI tutor you already use — there is no extra subscription to run ChemRender. The app is just the renderer that makes ochem explanations readable.
