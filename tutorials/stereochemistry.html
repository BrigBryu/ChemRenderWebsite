Tutorial

Studying stereochemistry

Chirality, stereocenters, enantiomers, and cis/trans geometry, rendered as wedge/dash cards with R/S and E/Z labels. You never write SMILES: the AI does it, you just study.

The concepts

  • Chirality / handedness. A molecule is chiral when it cannot be superimposed on its mirror image, like your left and right hands.
  • Stereocenter / chiral center. Usually a carbon with four different groups. ChemRender marks these and draws them with wedge and dash bonds.
  • Enantiomers. The two non-superimposable mirror images of a chiral molecule. They share every connection but differ in 3D handedness (R vs S).
  • E / Z geometry. The arrangement around a C=C double bond (the cis/trans idea, made precise).

Copy the Stereo Guide prompt

Inside the app, next to Prompt and Arrow Guide, there is a Stereo Guide button (or press y s). It copies an add-on prompt that tells your AI tutor how to encode 3D configuration in the SMILES. Paste it together with your chemistry question into any AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), then paste the answer back into ChemRender. You do not write SMILES; the AI does.

What you get back

  • Wedge / dash bonds at every stereocenter, drawn automatically.
  • R / S and E / Z labels printed right on the structure.
  • Stereocenters listed in a card's Details view; hover to find each chiral center.
  • One-click enantiomer. In Details, press Show enantiomer to flip the molecule to its mirror image and back.

Where the labels come from

ChemRender computes R/S and E/Z itself from the structure (using CIP priority rules), the same way it detects functional groups; they are not guessed by the AI. The AI only needs to encode the right 3D shape in the SMILES; the letters follow automatically. If a label ever looks wrong, ask your AI tutor to double-check the configuration in the SMILES.
